Semantic Layout Tags <header>

A header usually contains the site/page title, logo, or introduction. You can have a header for the page, and also headers inside sections/articles.

Tip: Click boxes to log which semantic tag you clicked.

What do these tags mean?

  • <nav> = main navigation links
  • <main> = the main unique content of the page (usually only one)
  • <section> = a themed group of content with a heading
  • <article> = a self-contained piece of content that could stand alone
  • <aside> = related content (sidebar, notes, links)
  • <footer> = footer info (copyright, contacts, related links)

Quick question to ask yourself: “Could I move this somewhere else and it still makes sense?” If yes, it might be an <article>.

A themed group of content <section>

Use a section when you have a chunk of content that belongs together under a heading.

A self-contained post <article>

An article is content that can stand on its own. Examples: a news story, a blog post, a product card.

Articles often have their own headings, author info, and dates.

A tiny example structure

<header>
  <h1>Site Title</h1>
  <nav>...links...</nav>
</header>

<main>
  <section>
    <h2>Topic</h2>
    <article>A standalone post</article>
  </section>

  <aside>Related links</aside>
</main>

<footer>Copyright</footer>

Related info <aside>

An aside is for content that is related but not the main focus.
